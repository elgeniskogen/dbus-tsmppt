Subject: TriStar MPPT - Questions about Daily Register Behavior and EEPROM Programming

Dear Morningstar Technical Support,

I am developing a driver for the TriStar MPPT solar charge controller using Modbus TCP communication. It is to be used on an off-grid cottage and should optimize the lifetime of Lithium Ion batteries. I am using TriStar MPPT Model: TS-MPPT-60 Firmware: Controller v1.1.42 and Server v9.25 and Serial #10170296 for the development.


During development, I have encountered some behaviors/questions that are not fully documented in the official Modbus specification, and I would appreciate clarification on the following points:


1. I would like to be able to program EEPROM parameters "on-the-fly" when needed, ideally also when the panels are in production. My guess is that best practice to avoid damage to the controller is to:

* Enable controller DISCONNECT
* Write to EEPROM
* Reset controller

Questions:
- Is the above the best practice?
- Is it OK to do this when in production?
- Is reset needed or can I just enable DISCONNECT before writing to EEPROM and then disable DISCONNECT after writing?


2. DAILY REGISTER RESET BEHAVIOR
   When the controller is reset (via COIL_RESET_CTRL), I observe that the daily energy registers (REG_WHC_DAILY at 0x003C) and daily max/min registers (REG_POUT_MAX_DAILY, REG_V_PV_MAX, REG_V_BAT_MAX, REG_V_BAT_MIN) and time accumulation registers (REG_T_ABS at 0x004D, REG_T_FLOAT at 0x004F, REG_T_EQ_DAILY at 0x004E) are all cleared to zero.

   Question:
   - Is this the expected behavior?

3. I experience that after a long time (month or longer) the comm server can stop responding, and I need to restart the controller. To avoid this I have set the driver to reset the comm server every night.

Question:
- Is this a known problem or is it only my test-rig that is malfunctioning?

4. When not doing other operations on Modbus, I am now polling 56 registers (0x0018 to 0x004F, addresses 24 to 79) every 5 seconds.

Question:
- What is the maximum polling frequency I can use and still have stable results?

5. I am experiencing that my controller is measuring the battery voltage with a 0.2V difference compared to a Victron BMV shunt. The TriStar is set up with voltage sense.

Question:
- Are there any ways to calibrate the voltage measurement on the TriStar? 


Thank you for your assistance. Any clarification on these points would be greatly helpful for ensuring robust and accurate monitoring.
